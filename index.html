Each card computes the best-fit size, the stem it'd take, and the stock crank that size ships with. Reach is corrected with stem length; stack you can raise with spacers but can't easily drop below the frame — so a too-tall frame is the real dealbreaker. Estimates, not a substitute for a fitting.

↕ Saddle height & crank length move together. A shorter crank drops the pedal at the bottom of the stroke, so raise your saddle by the same amount to keep leg extension — from 172.5 mm: 170 = +2.5 mm, 165 = +7.5 mm, 160 = +12.5 mm saddle. Each size here ships a different stock crank, so when a size-down also shortens the crank, plan to re-set saddle height. Your reference: 172.5 mm cranks, saddle ≈ 71 cm (BB → saddle top, approx. from the Journeyer demo).
