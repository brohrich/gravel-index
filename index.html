The natural experiment

My 2017 SuperSix (555/379, 73.1° head angle, ~28mm tires) and my Otso Waheela C (555/385, 71.5°, 32mm) are nearly identical in fit — yet I only descend confidently on the Otso. A shorter 80mm stem on the SuperSix didn't change that. So reach isn't the lever — descending confidence comes from a slacker head angle + bigger tires.

Reach is a stem, not a frame size

The Journeyer's 70–80mm stem put my hands ~40mm closer than 2017 at the same bar height. I can get a short cockpit at a sane size with a short stem — I don't need to size down for it (and sizing down mostly costs the stack/bar height I want to keep).

Saddle & crank move together

A shorter crank means raising the saddle by the same amount (172.5→165 = +7.5mm). Each frame size ships a different stock crank, so I check it per size.

Sizing down ≠ better descending

A smaller frame (Otso S vs M: −14 stack / −10 reach / −0.5° HTA) is a fit refinement, not a descending fix — the head angle & tire clearance that matter are shared across sizes. Worth a test ride, but it won't transform descending the way slack-HTA + big tires did.

Recommendations
  • Target ≤71° head angle + ≥50mm tires — at least as calm as my Otso (71.5° / 53mm). Sort by "Calmest descender" or filter ⛰ Calm descender.
  • GRX Di2 + "Calm" at my size: XDS XLAB GT8 & Ridley ASTR (~71° / 52–55mm). BMC Kaius too, but US builds are SRAM.
  • SRAM, "Calm" + huge tires: Allied Able & Lauf Seigla (70.5° / 57mm), Crux 5 (55mm); ENVE MOG is close ("Stable").
  • Watch the per-size head angle: Pinarello Grevil reads slack at size 50 but steepens to ~72° in my size — only "Balanced." Check the badge, not the brochure.
  • Avoid the steep aero frames (Áspero / Áspero-5, Grail, Kanzo Fast, Factor Ostro) — 42–45mm tires, nervous like the SuperSix.
  • Cheap test first: slacken the Otso Tuning Chip + run 40–45mm tires; test-ride a Small.
  • Book a fresh professional fit (overdue since 2017) before buying.

↕ Saddle height & crank length move together. A shorter crank drops the pedal at the bottom of the stroke, so raise your saddle by the same amount to keep leg extension — from 172.5 mm: 170 = +2.5 mm, 165 = +7.5 mm, 160 = +12.5 mm saddle. Each size here ships a different stock crank, so when a size-down also shortens the crank, plan to re-set saddle height.
